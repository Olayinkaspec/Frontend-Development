Dr. Benjamin Carson

"A Politician and an American retired Neurosurgeon who performed the first successful separation of occipital craniopagus twins in 1987."

Notable Things In The Life Of Dr. Benjamin Carson:

  1. Dr. Benjamin Solomon Carson, Sr. was the neurosurgeon that performed the first neurosurgical procedure on a fetus in the womb.

  2. He also performed the first successful separation of conjoined twins who were attached at the back of the head (occipital craniopagus twins.) in 1987.

  3. He refined a technique known as hemispherectomy, in which one-half of the brain is removed to prevent seizures in persons with severe epilepsy.

  4. As a politician, he served as the 17th United States Secretary of Housing and Urban Development from 2017 to 2021.

  5. He became the director of pediatric neurosurgery at the Johns Hopkins Children's Center in 1984 at age 33, then the youngest chief of pediatric neurosurgery in the United States.

  6. He was professor of neurosurgery, oncology, plastic surgery, and pediatrics at the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine.

  7. Dr. Carson gained national fame among political conservatives after delivering a speech at the 2013 National Prayer Breakfast which was perceived as critical of the policies of President Barack Obama.

  8. In 2001, he was named by CNN and Time magazine as one of the nation's 20 foremost physicians and scientists and was selected by the Library of Congress as one of 89 "Living Legends" on its 200th anniversary.

  9. In 2008, Dr. Carson was bestowed the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian award in the United States.

  10. In 2010, he was elected into the National Academy of Sciences Institute of Medicine, considered one of the highest honors in the fields of health and medicine.

  11. In 2012, Dr. Carson was the Influential Marylander Award recipient from The Daily Record, Baltimore's legal and business newspaper.
